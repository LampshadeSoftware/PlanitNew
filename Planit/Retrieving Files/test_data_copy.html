How Rastafari Moved World - 16030 - AFST 100 - 01
Rastafari cultural symbols are recognizable throughout the world and as a social and political movement, it has had an influence on language, music, film, and popular culture in Jamaica and beyond. We see its symbols represented all over the world, although we rarely have a good understanding of what they mean beyond the One Love that it expresses. This class is designed to give students a greater sense of the importance of the Rastafari philosophy beyond the outward symbols the world has adopted.

Black Speculative Arts/Afr Rel - 14534 - AFST 150 - 03
Black Speculation Arts and African Religious Imagination What are Black speculative arts? “Speculative fiction” generally refers to fantasy and science fiction, alternate visions of the past and the future, and the sliver of time that is the present. As Black speculative fiction writer Samuel R. Delany has said: “We need visions of the future, and our people need them more than most.” In this course, we expand from fiction to films, video, visual arts, music and performance art. We take “speculative” to mean both “to see” and “to think, or to wonder.” We include art that is fabulous, magical or other-worldly (African style), and also art that is about radical changes of view and viewpoint. Much of what we will experience is rooted in African religious imagination, ways of walking the worlds that “root” in Africa and pervade the places where African descendents live. This is a course about the human spirit and your own, the varieties of African religious genius and imagination, and the issues of race in 21st century America.

Registration Dates: Mar 19,2018 to Sep 07,2018
Levels: International Honours, Undergraduate
Attributes: College 150, First-Year Seminar, Lower Divsn Writing Req

Deciphering Ancient Egypt - 17169 - AFST 306 - 01
This course will explore the cultural history of ancient Egypt by reaching beyond kinglists and royal chronicles to gain insight into societal dynamics of class, gender, sexuality, and ethnicity from the rich visual repertoire of Egypt’s hieroglyphic writing system and art. Students will read Egyptian literary texts in translation but also develop the ability to read short statements and captions written in Egyptian hieroglyphs. Particular attention will be given to the Old and Middle Kingdoms, when the foundations of Egyptian art and literature were first established.
